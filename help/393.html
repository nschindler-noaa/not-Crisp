Keywords and descriptions for reaches

  • reach: Designates the beginning of a reach and its name.
  • width: Width of the reach in feet.
  • upper_depth: Depth in feet of the upstream end of the reach.
  • lower_depth: Depth in feet of the downstream end of the reach.
  • slope: The inward slope of the sides of the reach, in degrees.
  • lower_elev: Elevation above sea level of the river bottom at the lower end of each reach.
    Note. When elevation changes at dams are read from a flow archive file in Monte Carlo mode, they are propagated upstream until a reach is found whose "lower_elev" is higher than the full-pool elevation of the dam in question. That reach is not propagated, nor are any above it.
  • latlon: Provides latitude and longitude for points in the course of the reach, e.g. latlon 46 09 00 N 123 16 00 W marks 46 degrees 9 minutes north latitude, 123 degrees 16 minutes west longitude.
  • end (reach): Marks the end of a particular reach.
  • depth: Not recommended. Average depth in the reach, in feet. When possible, use the preferred parameters upper_depth and lower_depth.
